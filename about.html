About Me

Hello! I’m Camren Khoury, a third-year student at Clemson University majoring in Computer Engineering and Electrical Engineering. I have a deep passion for technology and how engineering can transform our world. My academic journey has provided me with a solid foundation in hardware and software, which I am eager to apply to real-world challenges.

Academic Journey

During my time at Clemson, I have grown my skills in several course areas including data structures, algorithms, electronics, and microcontroller systems. Highlights of my most recent school projects include:

  • Data Structures and Algorithms: Developing systems to manage and optimize data, including the design and debugging of memory heap management systems and linked lists.
  • Electronics and Circuit Design: Participating in hands-on labs working with tranistors, designing circuits, and using tools like curve tracers and oscilloscopes to problem solve.
  • Analog Signal Processing and Op-Amps: Using basic Op-Amps to control gain and work with larger electronic circuits like amplifiers, filters, and frequency response
  • Embedded Systems: Working with the PIC32MX150F128B Microcontroller to create various lab projects, including a Nuclear Meltdown Simulation.

I am planning on continue my learning of the C language, as well as any adjacent or industry-relevant languages. I am currently proficient in C and Python, and have experience with Apple Swift. The microcontrollers I have experience with are the PIC32, ESP32, and STM32
